## 1. Executive Summary & Vision

**StudioFlow** is not just a production management tool; it is the **central nervous system of modern filmmaking**. In an industry fragmented by disparate tools—spreadsheets for budgets, specialized software for scheduling, and endless email chains for communication—StudioFlow unifies the entire production lifecycle into a single, intelligent, and aesthetically stunning platform.

**Our Vision**: To empower creators with an **AI-native ecosystem** that anticipates needs, automates drudgery, and amplifies creativity. StudioFlow doesn't just track your production; it *understands* it. From the first draft of the script to the final cut, StudioFlow flows with you.

### Key Differentiators
- **Context-Aware AI**: The system knows your script, your budget, and your schedule. It suggests schedule optimizations based on script changes and alerts you to budget overruns before they happen.
- **Unified & Real-Time**: No more syncing. Changes in the script automatically update the breakdown, schedule, and potential budget impact.
- **Premium User Experience**: A "Dark Mode First" design philosophy that feels like a high-end cinematic tool, not a spreadsheet.

## 2. User Personas

### 1. The Visionary Director (Sarah)
*   **Goals**: visualizing safety, communicating creative intent, staying on creative track.
*   **Pain Points**: Disconnected tools, administrative overload distracting from art.
*   **StudioFlow Wins**: AI Storyboards generated from script text, instant moodboard creation, "Dailies Review" module integration.

### 2. The pragmatic Producer (David)
*   **Goals**: On time, on budget, happy crew.
*   **Pain Points**: Surprise costs, scheduling conflicts, communication bottlenecks.
*   **StudioFlow Wins**: Real-time "Budget Tracking" with alerts, "Schedule" optimization suggestions, automated "Call Sheets".

### 3. The Department Head (Elena - Production Design)
*   **Goals**: Executing the vision within constraints.
*   **Pain Points**: Last-minute script changes not reflecting in breakdown, chasing approvals.
*   **StudioFlow Wins**: "Asset Management" linked to specific scenes, "Collaboration Hub" for instant feedback, mobile-friendly scouting tools.

## 3. Functional Requirements

StudioFlow is built on a modular architecture, allowing productions to scale from indie shorts to blockbuster features.

### 3.1 Core Production Modules
*   **Dashboard**: A mission control center showing project health, upcoming tasks, and AI insights.
*   **Script**: The single source of truth. Features version control and auto-breakdown.
*   **Schedule**: Drag-and-drop stripboard with AI optimization for location moves and actor availability.
*   **Budget**: Detailed budgeting with industry-standard numbering (A-N). Dynamic linking to Schedule and Script.
*   **Cast & Crew**: Database management, deal memos, and availability tracking.

### 3.2 Creative Modules
*   **Storyboard**: AI-assisted visualization tools. Import scripts to generate shot lists and panels.
*   **Moodboard**: Collaborative visual space for textures, lighting, and reference imagery.
*   **Locations**: Scouting database with map integration and "sun path" tracking.
*   **Audio & VFX**: Specialized pipelines to track assets from creation to approval.

### 3.3 Logistics & Operations
*   **Call Sheets**: Automated generation based on the next day's schedule and crew lists.
*   **Gear Management**: Inventory tracking for camera, lighting, and grip departments.
*   **Legal**: Contract generation and digital signature tracking.
*   **Dailies**: Camera-to-cloud integration for reviewing footage on the go.

### 3.4 The "AI Genius" Layer
*   **Smart Alerts**: "Script change in Scene 4 requires practical VFX that is not budgeted."
*   **Generative Assist**: "Generate a call sheet for Day 4 based on these location constraints."
*   **Contextual Search**: "Show me all scenes with 'rain' that involve the 'Lead Actor'."

## 4. UI/UX Design System

*   **Aesthetic**: "Cinematic Dark". Deep charcoals, slate blues, and vibrant accent colors (Amber/Teal) for status indicators.
*   **Interaction**: Fluid animations, glassmorphism for overlays, and "command palette" navigation for power users.
*   **Responsiveness**: Fully functional on tablet for set use; distinct "mobile view" for quick approvals and call sheet checks.
